My Story

Ever since I was young, I had always dreamed of becoming a teacher. I liked school and the process of learning, so naturally I thought I would love being a teacher. However, after several years in the classroom, I felt constrained in my role and ultimately unfulfilled. This led me to explore the world of finance, where I taught myself the intricacies of money management. I passed the dreaded FINRA certifications and became a financial advisor. After some time in finance, I missed working as part of a team and collaborating with others.
I accepted a position as Children's Librarian and quickly advanced to the head of the department.
In this role, I started a coding program at my library and discovered my passion for coding. To further pursue this passion, I enrolled in a 3-month intensive Full Stack Development Bootcamp, based in Spain. Every day, I woke up at 2am to join my online cohort. Despite the early mornings, I was excited to get up each day to learn something new. I have come to realize that what I had always wanted was to be surrounded by knowledge, and I am thrilled to be in a field that allows me to continuously learn and grow.

Transition to Tech

When I decided to enroll in a bootcamp, I had some background in HTML/CSS and minimal experience with JavaScript. As someone who was used to excelling in my professional life, I was caught off guard by the difficulty of the first few weeks. An inspiring quote during orientation, "Be brave enough to suck at something new," really helped me push through the initial challenges. Despite feeling overwhelmed at the end of each day, I still found myself looking forward to the next day. As time went on, I began to truly enjoy the challenge and before I knew it, the bootcamp had ended and I was left wanting more. I am excited, motivated and passionate about continuing to learn and becoming a successful software engineer.
